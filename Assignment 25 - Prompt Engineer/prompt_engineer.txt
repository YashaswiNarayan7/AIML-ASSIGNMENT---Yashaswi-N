Prompt engineering is the process of designing effective inputs to guide AI models toward desired outputs. A well-structured prompt improves accuracy, relevance, and clarity, while a vague prompt produces generic or low-quality results.

1. Resume Generation
Weak Prompt

“Write a resume”

Strong Prompt

“Create a professional one-page resume for a final-year Computer Science Engineering student skilled in Python, SQL, Power BI, and data analysis. Include sections for education, technical skills, projects, and internships. Keep it concise and ATS-friendly.”

Comparison
Weak prompt lacks context and structure
Strong prompt specifies role, skills, format, and constraints
Output from strong prompt is more relevant and usable
2. Business Idea
Weak Prompt

“Give a business idea”

Strong Prompt

“Suggest a low-budget, scalable business idea suitable for college students in India, preferably using digital platforms. Include target audience, revenue model, and growth strategy.”

Comparison
Weak prompt leads to generic ideas
Strong prompt defines location, budget, and expectations
Output becomes practical and implementable
3. Study Plan
Weak Prompt

“Make a study plan”

Strong Prompt

“Create a 30-day study plan for a beginner to learn data analytics, covering SQL, Python, and Power BI. Allocate daily tasks with time estimates and include revision and practice sessions.”

Comparison
Weak prompt is vague and open-ended
Strong prompt includes duration, subjects, and structure
Output becomes structured and actionable
Key Principles of Strong Prompts
Clarity – Clearly define what you want
Context – Provide background information
Specificity – Mention constraints (time, format, level)
Structure – Specify output format
Goal-Oriented – Define the final objective
